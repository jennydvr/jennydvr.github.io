BIO PROJECTS

Red Dead Redemption 2

RDR2 is a western-themed action adventure game, published in October 2018 for PS4 and Xbox One. The game was acclaimed by critics, who praised the story, characters, technology, and considerable level of detail.

The game was developed using RAGE, Rockstar's game engine. As part of the UI team, my main responsibilities are to maintain and extend RAGE's UI systems, implement design prototypes, iterate over them to get intuitive UIs and cooperate with other teams to make sure we take advantage of the capabilities our systems have.

Klaus

KLAUS is a platform-puzzle 2D game developed for PS4 released in January 2016, using Unity3D.

I was in charge of developing the UI/UX, leaderboards, connection to the Playstation Network and the AI and gameplay for the bosses.

The Librarian

This game was my project for the Master in Creation of Videogames (Universitat Pompeu Fabra, 2016). It was developed from scratch using C++ and Lua following a data oriented design.

My team was made up of 4 artists and 4 programmers. I developed the gameplay and AI systems, character control, cameras behaviors and the animation system, and I played an active role in the design of the game.

We were awarded a prize for Best Universitary Game in the X National Awards of the Videogame Industry in Spain 2017.

Play on Gamejolt


El Canto del Autana

El Canto del Autana (The Song of the Autana) is a 3D platformer developed for PC using Unity3D, which was released in 2014. The game shows the geography of Venezuela and the plot is based on aboriginal myths of the country.

Part of my work included developing the UI/UX of the game (lifebars, minimaps, minigames UI, tutorials, options and other in-game menus), game optimization and support the development of gameplay systems.

Competitor in the IEEE Latin American Robotics Competition

Celebrated in Brazil 2012, the goal of this competition was to create an autonomous robot looking for garbage around a sand arena, collect it and put out the garbage in a depot.

I participated along with three other classmates, and we used an XBC as the main controller of the robot. We used OpenCV for the image processing, which were being captured by a camera we had attached to the robot.
